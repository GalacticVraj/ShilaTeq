## Overview

Most software a stone yard might buy falls into one of three buckets: a **paper register** (free, familiar, and blind), **generic accounting software** (books-first, stone-blind), or a **generic ERP** (powerful, expensive, and built for factories that make identical widgets). None of them model a *stone block* — an irregular, high-value, one-of-a-kind object that gets cut into slabs, leaves a remnant, ages on the lot, and carries a margin nobody can see.

ShilaTeq's central strength is that it is **purpose-built for the physics and economics of stone**, and delivered in a form a low-literacy, offline, cash-and-credit business can actually use. This page lays out those strengths, grouped by theme, each framed as a business advantage with the "why it wins" angle.

> **The one-line differentiator:** ShilaTeq is the only system that gives *every stone block a digital identity* and runs the *entire* yard business — intake to delivered invoice — on any phone, offline, in the worker's own language, with India-native GST and WhatsApp built in.

## 🏆 Best Features (The Signature Capabilities)

These are the capabilities that define the product — the things a prospective customer sees and immediately understands are not available anywhere else.

### 1. QR digital identity on every block
Every block gets a permanent QR-coded identity the instant it's tagged. Scan it with a phone camera and its dimensions, grade, cost, age, status, and history appear in seconds. **Why it wins:** it replaces "walk the yard and hope" with "point, scan, done" — the founding 10-second promise, and the anchor for everything else. ✅ Confirmed

### 2. The partial-cut engine (block → slabs → remnant)
When a block is cut, ShilaTeq captures the *whole event*: how much material was consumed, what slabs came out, the **recovery percentage**, the **wastage**, and — critically — the **remnant block** left over, which is instantly sellable again. Every cut writes an immutable ledger entry. **Why it wins:** cutting is where stone yards silently leak money; ShilaTeq is the only tool that reconciles output against input and turns the leftover into tracked, quotable inventory. ✅ Confirmed

### 3. Offline-first shop floor
Workers log cutting output and task progress even with no signal; actions save on-device and sync automatically, safely, and without duplicates when connectivity returns. **Why it wins:** the shop floor is exactly where signal dies — this is what makes the software usable by the people who do the physical work, instead of an office-only tool. ✅ Confirmed

### 4. Public 3D showroom that captures leads
A per-yard, WebGL-powered 3D catalog gives even a small yard a premium online shopfront. Prices are gated per block ("On request" where the yard prefers), and every visitor can request a quote that lands in the admin inbox. **Why it wins:** it's not just a website — it's a lead-generation funnel that turns anonymous web traffic into tracked, followable enquiries. ✅ Confirmed

### 5. Bilingual, low-literacy worker UX
The worker app runs in **English or हिंदी**, is icon- and number-first, and is designed for people who can't (or don't want to) read dense English ERP screens. **Why it wins:** adoption. The best system in the world is worthless if the cutter won't touch it — ShilaTeq meets the workforce where it is. ✅ Confirmed

### 6. India-native GST & WhatsApp commerce
One-tap **GST invoices** with correct CGST/SGST/IGST splits, HSN codes, and rupee amounts spelled out in Indian words — plus **WhatsApp** as the built-in channel for quotes, invoices, tracking, and reminders. **Why it wins:** it's built for the *exact* market it serves. Compliance and communication that would take a generic tool weeks of configuration are one tap here. ✅ Confirmed

## 🎯 Competitive Advantages

### Domain depth nothing generic can match
ShilaTeq models the stone itself — volumetric (area-based) pricing, block-and-slab lifecycles, recovery-adjusted costing, remnants, aging, carrying cost. A generic ERP treats a block like a boxed SKU; ShilaTeq knows it's an irregular, divisible, aging asset. **This is a moat:** replicating it requires understanding the trade, not just writing code.

### One system, not five
Procurement, inventory, manufacturing, sales, finance, payroll, dispatch, and a public storefront live in **one platform** with one login and one source of truth. The alternative — a register for stock, a spreadsheet for money, a separate accounting package, WhatsApp for everything else — leaves gaps where money and stock fall through.

### India-native from the ground up
₹ formatting, GST tax model, HSN 6802, amount-in-words, Hindi language, WhatsApp channel, cash-and-credit workflows. This isn't a Western ERP with a currency setting flipped — it's shaped around how Indian stone yards actually trade.

### Zero-infrastructure, zero-lock-in start
The product runs on services the yard already implicitly has (a browser, a phone camera) plus a managed cloud — no servers, no installs, no per-message fees, no payment-processor cut. Data exports freely to Excel/CSV, so nothing is trapped. Low switching cost in; no lock-in out.

### A demo that needs nothing
ShilaTeq ships with a **full in-browser demo mode** — realistic seed data, resettable, no server required. **Why it wins:** sales and onboarding are frictionless; a prospect can explore the entire product on the spot, and a new user can practice without touching real data.

## 🤖 Automation Capabilities

ShilaTeq automates the error-prone, repetitive glue between steps:

- **Auto-generated documents & codes** — invoice numbers, order/quote/PO codes, block codes, gate passes, and QR labels are all minted automatically. ✅ Confirmed
- **Derived money math** — an order's paid amount, outstanding balance, and a customer's store-credit balance are computed automatically from the underlying payments and ledger, so they can never drift out of sync. ✅ Confirmed
- **State transitions that cascade** — a delivered dispatch automatically completes its order to "sold," snapshots cost of goods, and closes the related worker assignments. ✅ Confirmed
- **Recovery-adjusted costing** — cutting wastage automatically raises the per-unit cost of the slabs produced, so margins reflect reality without anyone doing the math. ✅ Confirmed
- **Aging & carrying-cost alerts** — blocks silently age into amber and red buckets with carrying-cost estimates, surfaced as alerts. ✅ Confirmed
- **Lead status progression** — replying to a showroom enquiry on WhatsApp auto-advances it from "new" to "contacted." ✅ Confirmed
- **Automatic live sync** — screens refresh themselves across devices and windows; no manual reload. ✅ Confirmed
- **Automatic offline sync** — queued shop-floor actions drain and reconcile the moment signal returns. ✅ Confirmed

**Why it wins:** automation here isn't a gimmick — it removes exactly the manual steps where a busy yard makes costly mistakes (wrong tax, drifted balances, double-sold stock, forgotten write-offs).

## 🎨 User Experience Strengths

- **Mobile-first, phone-shaped.** Built for the mid-range Android in a worker's pocket, not a desktop retrofit.
- **Two tailored surfaces.** A rich admin app for owners/supervisors and a stripped-down, tap-based worker app — each shows a role exactly what it needs and nothing it doesn't.
- **Bilingual and icon-first.** English/हिंदी with number- and icon-led design so literacy is never a barrier.
- **Never-breaks resilience.** The 3D showroom falls back to a CSS animation; stale-deploy errors self-heal with a reload; offline actions reassure rather than error. The product degrades gracefully instead of failing.
- **Fast even on modest hardware.** Heavy features load only when needed; lists stay light; screens show shaped placeholders instead of blank spinners, so nothing jumps around when data lands.
- **Zero-friction demo & practice.** New users can explore and rehearse in demo mode with no risk to real data.
- **Print that just works.** GST invoices, quotations, gate passes, and QR labels print cleanly from the browser with no plugins.

**Why it wins:** adoption and trust. A tool that's fast, forgiving, and speaks the user's language gets used every day — which is the only way an operating system pays off.

## 📈 Scalability Advantages

- **Multi-tenant by design.** Every yard's data is isolated at the database level; the same platform serves many yards securely from one deployment. ✅ Confirmed
- **Managed cloud backend.** Storage, auth, realtime, and compute scale as a managed service — no servers for the yard or the operator to grow into. ✅ Confirmed
- **Global edge delivery.** The app loads fast wherever users are, on whatever network. ✅ Confirmed
- **Efficient by construction.** Live freshness is event-driven rather than constant polling; large photo payloads are kept out of list views; exports and heavy 3D load on demand. The platform stays responsive as data grows. ✅ Confirmed
- **Concurrency-safe operations.** Stock reservations and worker task steps are protected against double-booking and race conditions, so multiple users acting at once can't corrupt inventory or money. ✅ Confirmed

> **⚠️ Limitation (honest note):** Today the platform is strongest as **one yard per account**. Cross-yard, multi-branch *organisation rollups* (a group owner seeing all their yards in one view) are limited. This is a clear growth path, not a wall — see [Product Opportunities](12_Product_Opportunities.md).

## 💎 Differentiators — The "Hidden Gems"

These are the deep, hard-to-copy capabilities that reward a closer look — the details that separate ShilaTeq from anything a yard could assemble from off-the-shelf parts.

| Differentiator | Why it wins |
|---|---|
| **Recovery-adjusted costing & COGS snapshots** | Wastage raises unit cost automatically, and the true cost of goods is frozen at the moment of sale — so a later price edit can never rewrite the history of past margins. Books that tell the truth. |
| **Store-credit ledger** | Overpayments and returns become a tracked store-credit balance that can be applied to future orders — money that used to quietly vanish is now an asset on the customer's account, with no double-counting. |
| **Concurrency-safe reservations** | Two users can't sell the same block; the system reserves all-or-nothing and tells the loser to refresh. No oversold stock, ever. |
| **Immutable cut-event ledger** | Every cut is permanently recorded — a tamper-proof manufacturing history for yield, disputes, and analysis. |
| **Attendance wage snapshots** | A day's wage is frozen when marked, so changing a worker's rate later never rewrites past payroll. Clean, defensible pay history. |
| **Live sync (realtime + polling belt-and-braces)** | Two independent freshness mechanisms mean screens stay current even if one path hiccups. Reliability by redundancy. |
| **Typo-tolerant search** | Finds a block despite a spelling slip — but never fuzzy-matches a code (so `KSH-B0002` can't be mistaken for `KSH-B0001`). Forgiving where it should be, exact where it must be. |
| **Zero-infra demo mode** | The whole product runs in the browser with realistic data and a reset button — instant demos, safe practice, no setup. |
| **Print suite** | Four self-contained printers (GST invoice with amount-in-words, quotation, gate pass with tracking QR, block QR label) that produce real yard paperwork with no dependencies. |
| **CSV hardening** | Exports neutralize spreadsheet formula-injection and preserve the ₹ symbol in Excel — safe, correct data every time. |
| **Procedural stone artwork** | Showroom imagery is generated as crisp, lightweight graphics rather than heavy photos — fast, offline-safe, and always available. |
| **Public dispatch tracking** | A safe, price-free tracking link lets a customer follow their delivery — a premium touch small yards never had. |
| **Order editing under reservation** | A reserved order can have lines added or removed with stock automatically re-reserved or released, floored at what's already been paid. Flexibility without leaks. |

> **💡 Tip:** Any one of these would be a headline feature elsewhere. Together they signal a product built by people who understand both the trade and the software — depth that's very hard for a fast-follower to replicate.

## Why It Wins — In One Paragraph

ShilaTeq wins because it refuses the two easy paths. It doesn't ask a stone yard to pretend a block is a boxed product (the ERP trap), and it doesn't settle for tracking only the money after the fact (the accounting trap). Instead it models the stone, the money, the people, and the customer as one connected system — and then wraps that depth in an experience a Hindi-speaking cutter can use offline on a cheap phone, an owner can trust for GST and margins, and a buyer can discover through a 3D shopfront. Depth where the trade needs it; simplicity where the user needs it; India-native where the market needs it. That combination is the moat.
